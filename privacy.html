🥟 Pastizzi Crush — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2 July 2026

The short version: Pastizzi Crush does not collect personal data. Your game progress stays on your device. The only thing ever sent online is the nickname, avatar emoji and score you choose to show on the leaderboard.

1. Who we are

Pastizzi Crush is a casual match-3 game published by Terence Camilleri. Contact: terencecamilleripesci@gmail.com.

2. Data stored on your device only

Level progress, stars, coins, lives, boosters, settings (sound/music) and your local scores are stored in your device's local storage. They never leave your device and are deleted if you uninstall the app or clear its data.

3. Leaderboard data

If you play levels, the game submits your chosen nickname (max 12 characters), your chosen avatar emoji and your score to our leaderboard database, hosted in the European Union (Supabase, Frankfurt). This is shown publicly in the in-game leaderboard. Do not use your real name in your nickname if you don't want it displayed. No account, email, device identifier, or any other personal information is collected or stored.

4. What we do NOT do

The game contains no advertising, no analytics, no tracking, no third-party SDKs collecting data, and no in-app purchases. We do not sell, share, or process personal data.

5. Children

The game is suitable for all ages. Because we collect no personal data, no parental consent mechanisms are required. Nicknames are free-text chosen by the player; we recommend children use a made-up name.

6. Data removal

To remove a leaderboard entry, email terencecamilleripesci@gmail.com with the nickname and we will delete it. All other data can be removed by uninstalling the app.

7. Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will be published at this same address.
